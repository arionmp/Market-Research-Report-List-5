<p><p>Avalanche Photodiodes (APDs) are types of photodetectors that operate in a high-gain mode by undergoing avalanche multiplication when biased above their breakdown voltage. There are two main types of APDs in the market: Silicon-based APDs (Si-APD) and Indium Gallium Arsenide-based APDs (InGaAs-APD). Si-APDs are widely used in visible light applications due to their low cost and high quantum efficiency. In contrast, InGaAs-APDs are commonly used in near-infrared applications due to their high sensitivity and low noise characteristics.</p></p>
