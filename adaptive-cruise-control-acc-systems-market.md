<p><p>Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) systems utilize various sensor technologies to enhance vehicle automation and safety. Infrared sensors detect heat emissions from surrounding vehicles, providing real-time distance measurements. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to gauge proximity, useful for low-speed scenarios. Laser sensors, employing light pulses, offer precise distance and speed data for effective tracking of nearby vehicles. Other market types may include radar and camera-based systems, each contributing uniquely to the overall functionality and reliability of ACC systems.</p></p>
