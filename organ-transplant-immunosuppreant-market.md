<p><p>The organ transplant immunosuppressant market comprises several key types. Selective inhibitors of cytokine production and function target specific immune responses to reduce rejection rates. Immunosuppressive antimetabolites disrupt DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing immune cells, effectively diminishing their activity. Immunosuppressive antibodies, often derived from monoclonal technologies, target specific immune components, providing targeted suppression. Immunosuppressive adrenocorticosteroids reduce inflammation and immune responses, offering broad-spectrum immunosuppression. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing transplant success by mitigating organ rejection.</p></p>
