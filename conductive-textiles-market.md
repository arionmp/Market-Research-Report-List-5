<p><p>Conductive textiles are materials woven, non-woven, or knitted with conductive fibers or coatings to enable the transmission of electrical signals. Woven textiles are traditional fabrics made by interlacing yarns at right angles, offering durability and flexibility. Non-woven textiles are made by bonding or felting fibers together without weaving, providing cost-effective solutions with lower strength. Knitted textiles are created by interlocking loops of yarn, offering stretchability and breathability. Each market type caters to different needs in industries such as fashion, healthcare, and electronics.</p></p>
