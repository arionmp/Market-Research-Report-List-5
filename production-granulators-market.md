<p><ul><li>Dry Granulation</li><li>Wet Granulation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The production granulators market encompasses two primary types: dry granulation and wet granulation. Dry granulation involves compressing powder into granules without the use of liquids, ideal for heat-sensitive materials. This method is efficient for producing uniform granules and requires less processing time. In contrast, wet granulation utilizes liquid binders to form granules, enhancing particle size consistency and improving flow characteristics. This method is often preferred for pharmaceutical applications due to its ability to enhance drug solubility and bioavailability.</p></p>
