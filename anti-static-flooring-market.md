Resilient anti-static flooring, such as vinyl or rubber, offers flexibility and comfort while effectively dissipating static electricity, making it suitable for environments like laboratories and manufacturing facilities. Non-resilient anti-static flooring, typically composed of materials like tile or concrete, provides a more rigid surface ideal for areas requiring high durability and low maintenance, often found in data centers and electronic assembly environments.</p></p>
