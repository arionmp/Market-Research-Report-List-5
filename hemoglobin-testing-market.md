<p><p>Hemoglobin testing involves various methods, primarily chromatography and immunoassays. Chromatography separates components in a sample based on their interactions with a stationary phase. This method allows for precise measurement of hemoglobin variants and is widely used in diagnosing conditions like sickle cell disease. Immunoassays utilize antibodies to detect specific proteins in the blood, offering rapid and sensitive results. Both types of testing are essential for monitoring hemoglobin levels and diagnosing related disorders, ensuring effective patient management.</p></p>
