<p><p>The bio-based ethylene market includes three primary types: sugars, starch, and lignocellulosic biomass. Sugars, derived from sources like sugarcane and sugar beets, can be fermented to produce ethylene. Starch, often sourced from corn and potatoes, undergoes hydrolysis to release glucose for fermentation. Lignocellulosic biomass, obtained from plant materials such as wood and agricultural residues, offers a sustainable route by breaking down cellulose and hemicellulose into fermentable sugars. Each type presents unique processing methods and sustainability benefits for ethylene production.</p></p>
