<p><p>The automotive air suspension systems market features two primary types: manual air suspension and electronic air suspension. Manual air suspension relies on a traditional setup where the driver controls air pressure via valves and a compressor, offering simpler functionality and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, electronic air suspension utilizes advanced sensors and actuators to automatically adjust air pressure for improved ride quality and stability. This system enhances comfort by adapting to road conditions dynamically, making it increasingly popular in high-end vehicles.</p></p>
