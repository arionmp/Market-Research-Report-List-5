<p><strong>The Live Cell RNA Detection Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Linear Oligonucleotide Probe</li><li>Linear FRET Probe</li><li>Autoligation FRET Probe</li><li>Molecular Beacon</li><li>MS2-GFP</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Live Cell RNA Detection market includes several types of probes for monitoring RNA in living cells. Linear oligonucleotide probes are short, single-stranded DNA or RNA sequences that bind specifically to target RNA, while linear FRET probes utilize fluorescence resonance energy transfer to provide enhanced sensitivity. Autoligation FRET probes incorporate a self-ligation mechanism for improved signal detection. Molecular beacons are dual-labeled probes that emit fluorescence upon target binding, and MS2-GFP systems employ a viral RNA-binding protein paired with green fluorescent protein for visualizing RNA in live cells.</p></p>
