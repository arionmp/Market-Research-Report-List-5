<p><p>The specular microscopes market comprises two key types: contact and non-contact specular microscopes. Contact specular microscopes involve direct contact with the sample's surface, allowing detailed imaging of cell structure and morphology. They are typically used for eye examinations and corneal assessments. In contrast, non-contact specular microscopes utilize advanced optical systems to capture images without physical interaction, making them ideal for sensitive samples. This type ensures minimal disturbance, making it suitable for a broader range of applications, including research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
