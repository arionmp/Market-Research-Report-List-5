<p><p>The contraceptive drugs market is categorized into three main types: long-acting, short-acting, and emergency contraceptives. Long-acting contraceptives, such as IUDs and implants, provide protection for extended periods, often years. Short-acting contraceptives, including pills and patches, require regular administration, typically daily or weekly. Emergency contraceptives are used post-intercourse to prevent pregnancy and are available in various forms, such as pills. Each type caters to different needs and preferences, influencing market dynamics and consumer choices.</p></p>
