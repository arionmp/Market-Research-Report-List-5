<p><ul><li>Low Temperature Furnace</li><li>High Temperature Furnace</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The debinding furnace market includes low temperature and high temperature furnaces, each serving specific applications. Low temperature furnaces are designed for removing binders from green parts, generally operating below 600°C, making them suitable for delicate materials that require gentle processing. In contrast, high temperature furnaces operate above 600°C, capable of handling more robust materials and allowing for the complete removal of complex binders. Both types are crucial in industries like metal injection molding and ceramics, ensuring optimal product integrity.</p></p>
