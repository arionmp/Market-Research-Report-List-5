<p><p>The bioabsorbable nail market includes two primary types: plastics and metals. Plastic bioabsorbable nails, often made from polymers like polylactic acid (PLA), offer a lightweight, corrosion-resistant option that gradually dissolves in the body. Metal bioabsorbable nails, typically composed of magnesium or iron alloys, provide superior strength and support during healing, eventually breaking down into biocompatible substances. Both types are used in orthopedic surgeries to promote seamless recovery while minimizing the need for removal procedures and reducing long-term complications.</p></p>
